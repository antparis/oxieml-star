## 1. The debate, three camps (still live in 2026)
The status of weak values has been contested since 1988 and is NOT settled. Three positions:
 - REALIST (Vaidman, Two-State Vector Formalism / TSVF): the weak value is a property of a single
   pre- and post-selected system; the future boundary condition carries physical content. Vaidman's
   own review notes the polemic "never stopped" and the literature has roughly doubled.
 - STATISTICAL (Ferrie & Combes 2014): anomalous weak values are artefacts of post-selection on rare
   events; nothing strange. (The camp our 'is it just a selection artefact?' worry maps onto.)
 - OPERATIONAL: weak values are useful calculational tools, ontology agnostic; they predict pointer
   shifts regardless of interpretation.
## 2. What contextuality changed (strengthens the 'not a mere artefact' side)
Pusey 2014 (PRL 113, 200401; arXiv:1409.1535): anomalous weak values are PROOFS OF CONTEXTUALITY --
non-classical in a precise, rigorous sense (incompatible with non-contextual ontological models). This
is a theorem, since demonstrated experimentally. Kunjwal-Lostaglio-Pusey 2019 (PRA 100, 042116;
arXiv:1812.06940) extend it to robustness, tightness, and the IMAGINARY parts. A 2026 result (Zhao et
al., Chinese Phys. B 35, 020301) drops the infinitesimal-disturbance approximation and finds anomalous
weak values NECESSARY for contextuality, extended to multi-photon. Net effect: the pure 'statistical
artefact' reading is weakened; there is genuine non-classicality, including in the imaginary part --
which is exactly our anti-holomorphic component.
## 3. The point that DIRECTLY validates our negative result on the arrow of time
Dressel & Jordan 2012 (PRA 85, 012107; arXiv:1112.3986), reaffirmed 2025: the IMAGINARY part of the
weak value does NOT inform about the measured observable; it is the logarithmic directional derivative
of the post-selection probability along the UNITARY flow generated by the observable. I.e. Im(W) is tied
to UNITARY (REVERSIBLE) disturbance. This is, independently and from the established literature, exactly
what we found today (be413fef): the holo/anti structure lives in the REVERSIBLE sector (unitary phase),
NOT in the irreversible arrow of time. Our negative result is not a quirk of our framework -- it agrees
with the standard operational reading of Im(W). External validation.
## 4. Our convention is the TSVF convention (not arbitrary)
We assumed the bra / backward branch carries the conjugate (tbar). In TSVF the future state <Phi| is
literally the Hermitian conjugate of the state evolving BACKWARD in time from the post-selected final
state (e.g. arXiv:1407.4613). So our 'assumed convention' is the standard TSVF definition.
